Agriculture in Nepal has long been based on subsistence farming, particularly in the hilly regions where peasants derive their living from fragmented plots of land cultivated in difficult conditions. Government programs to introduce irrigation facilities and fertilizers have proved inadequate, their delivery hampered by the mountainous terrain. Population increases and environmental degradation have ensured that the minimal gains in agricultural production, owing more to the extension of arable land than to improvements in farming practices, have been cancelled out. Once an exporter of rice, Nepal now has a food deficit.

Agriculture sector engages around 66% of the total population in Nepal but it hasn't enhanced the economy of the country so far because people are mostly engaged in subsistence farming. The contribution of agriculture to the GDP of Nepal was 26.21 in the fiscal year 2019/20 and increased to 19.9% in 2020/21.

Some initiatives devised by Nepal Government to achieve the goals of making agriculture a reliable economic sector in nation's growth.

Agriculture Development Strategy (ADS): The Agriculture Development Strategy (ADS) is a comprehensive initiative launched by the Government of Nepal in 2015 to promote sustainable agricultural growth, reduce poverty, and increase food security in the country. The strategy aims to improve the livelihoods of farmers by increasing agricultural productivity, enhancing market access, and promoting modern farming practices. The ADS also prioritizes sustainable agriculture practices to mitigate the impact of climate change on agriculture. The strategy is implemented through various government agencies and institutions, including the Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock Development, the Department of Agriculture, and the Nepal Agricultural Research Council. The ADS is a long-term plan that aims to improve the agriculture sector in Nepal over the next 15 years.
Agriculture Perspective Plan (APP): The Agriculture Perspective Plan (APP) is a long-term plan developed by the Government of Nepal to achieve inclusive and sustainable agricultural growth until 2030. The plan aims to promote agricultural growth, increase farmers' income, and improve food security in the country. The APP focuses on increasing agricultural productivity by promoting modern farming practices, developing appropriate technologies, and improving irrigation and other infrastructure. The plan also emphasizes the need to develop a strong value chain for agricultural products to ensure farmers receive a fair price for their produce. The APP is implemented through various government agencies and institutions, including the Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock Development, the Department of Agriculture, and the Nepal Agricultural Research Council.
National Agriculture Policy: The National Agriculture Policy is a policy framework developed by the Government of Nepal to guide the development of the agriculture sector in the country. The policy aims to improve the living standards of farmers, increase agricultural productivity, and promote sustainable agriculture practices. The policy recognizes the critical role of agriculture in the country's economy and emphasizes the need to promote inclusive and sustainable agricultural growth. The policy is implemented through various government agencies and institutions, including the Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock Development, the Department of Agriculture, and the Nepal Agricultural Research Council.
Nepal Agriculture Research Council (NARC): The Nepal Agriculture Research Council (NARC) is a government institution responsible for developing new technologies and practices for sustainable agriculture in Nepal. The council aims to improve the quality of seeds and fertilizers, promote effective extension services to farmers, and enhance the capacity of the agriculture sector to adapt to climate change. NARC also collaborates with other research institutions and organizations to promote agricultural research and development in the country.
Agriculture Inputs Company (AIC): The Agriculture Inputs Company (AIC) is a government-owned company responsible for ensuring the timely supply of quality agricultural inputs, such as seeds, fertilizers, and pesticides, to farmers in Nepal. The company aims to provide these inputs to farmers at reasonable prices to support their agricultural production. AIC also works to improve the availability and accessibility of agricultural inputs to farmers across the country.
Community-based Agriculture and Rural Development Project (CBARDP): The Community-based Agriculture and Rural Development Project (CBARDP) is an initiative launched by the Government of Nepal to improve the living standards of rural communities through sustainable agriculture and rural development. The project aims to enhance the capacity of rural communities to engage in sustainable agriculture practices, develop appropriate infrastructure, and promote access to finance and other support services. CBARDP works with local communities, government agencies, and other stakeholders to promote sustainable agriculture and rural development in Nepal.
